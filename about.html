Our Beginnings

JAC Records was founded in 2017 by Joel Perez, Armond Chatman and Chris Coakley. The three met in high school, and became friends bonded by a common passion -- music. Unfortunetly, music couldn't save them from stirring up mischef, and they were expelled from Schenectady High School after a prank on staff memebers went wrong in 2015. For more than two years, the three friends continued to hang out and work dead-end jobs, but all wanted more out of life. Armond was an aspiring musician with a decent local following, but couldn't garner the attention of mid-sized or even small record labels. Joel and Chris knew quite a bit about the music and entertainment industry, as Joel was the son of singer and actress Rosie Perez, and Chris's uncle Tim Coakley was a well-known jazz drummer and enthusiast. Wanting to help Armond reach his goals and make something of their own lives, Chris and Joel decided they wanted to start a record label. However, when they approached Armond with the idea, he didn't want their help. He wanted in. So with that, JAC Records -- or at least the concept -- was born. Today, JAC Records is one of the top small labels in New York State, and continue to help local artists reach their full potential.
